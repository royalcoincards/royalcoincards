Coin Card Manufacturer in Pakistan: The Complete Guide

6 min readRoyal Coin Cards TeamKarachi, Pakistan

If you sell gold or silver coins and bars in Pakistan, the packaging your customer takes home matters almost as much as what's inside it. That's the job of a coin card manufacturer — a company that designs, prints, and seals the blister card packaging jewellers and bullion dealers use to present their coins. This guide explains how that industry works in Pakistan, and what separates a serious manufacturer from a reseller.

What does a coin card manufacturer actually make?

A coin card manufacturer doesn't touch the metal at all. Our job starts and ends with the packaging: a printed card backing, usually foil stamped with a brand's logo and colours, paired with a clear plastic blister capsule that holds the coin or bar securely in place. The two are heat-sealed together into a single tamper-evident unit. Everything else — the coin, its weight, its certification — belongs entirely to the jeweller or bullion dealer who orders the cards.

Why local manufacturing beats imported packaging

For years, premium coin card packaging in Pakistan was almost entirely imported — ordered from suppliers abroad with long lead times, minimum orders that didn't suit smaller jewellers, and zero room for local customisation. That gap is exactly why Royal Coin Cards was built: to manufacture this packaging inside Pakistan, at wholesale scale, with full control over design.

The practical difference this makes

  • Faster turnaround — no international shipping or customs delays between design approval and delivery.
  • Lower minimum orders — local production means smaller jewellers can order without importing a container-sized batch.
  • True customisation — your logo, your colours, your card theme, proofed and adjusted before printing.
  • Direct communication — WhatsApp a question and get an answer the same day, not through an overseas agent.

What to look for in a wholesale coin card supplier

Not every supplier calling themselves a "manufacturer" actually produces cards in-house. Before ordering from any coin card packaging supplier in Pakistan, it's worth checking a few things:

1. Do they control the full production process?

Ask whether printing, foil stamping, die-cutting, and blister sealing all happen under one roof. A supplier who outsources pieces of the process to different vendors has less control over consistency — and usually a longer lead time.

2. Can they show you a physical sample before your order?

A manufacturer confident in their print quality and seal integrity will happily send a sample or a digital proof before you commit to a production run.

3. What's their minimum order quantity?

MOQs vary by card format. Reasonable suppliers will have a starting MOQ (often around 500 pieces) with room to negotiate for repeat customers.
